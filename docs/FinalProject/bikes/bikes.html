My first motorcycle was a bicycle frame I cut apart and welded in a Harbor Freight cement mixer engine in junior year of highschool. It was one of the ugliest, most dangerous things I have ever ridden, but holy shit it was fun.

I now work at House of Motorrad, a small motorcycle repair and rental shop here in Boulder, where I am honing my skills and very slowly building out my toolset, both physical and skill wise.

Some projects you see above include a turbocharged Ninja 500, heavily modified ninja 250, even more heavily modified VTR1000f, an Aprilia Falco, and a street legal YZ450. There are many more bike projects I have worked on and many more to come :)

Please check out my contact page to reach me about any motorcycle or fabrication related questions or if you just want to say hi.
